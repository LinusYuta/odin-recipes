Mapo Tofu

Description

This Vegan Mapo Tofu is spicy, saucy, and seriously addictive — the ultimate comfort food for when you're craving bold, umami-packed flavor. Inspired by the dish I always ordered at Chinese restaurants and my mom’s cozier version, this one hits the perfect balance of heat and heart.

Ingredients

  • Silken or Soft Tou
  • Dried Shitake Mushrooms
  • Doubanjiang
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Soy Sauce
  • Cornstarch
  • Water
  • Sichuan Peppers
  • Chili Flakes
  • Green Onions
  • Sesame Oil

Steps

  1. Soak the mushrooms: Rehydrate the dried shiitake mushrooms in warm water for at least 20 minutes. Reserve 200ml of the soaking water for the sauce.
  2. Cut the tofu into cubes
  3. Finely mince the garlic and grate the ginger.
  4. Slice the mushrooms into small pieces.
  5. Thinly slice the scallions, separating the white and green parts.
  6. In a pot of salted boiling water, blanch the tofu cubes for 3 minutes. Gently strain and set aside.
  7. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the mushrooms and stir-fry until lightly golden.
  8. Add the garlic, ginger, and white parts of the scallions. Sauté for another 1–2 minutes.
  9. Stir in doubanjiang, soy sauce, chili flakes, sugar, Sichuan peppercorns, and the reserved mushroom water. Mix well and bring to a gentle boil.
  10. Gently add in the tofu cubes, spooning sauce over to coat.
  11. Once the sauce comes to a boil, stir the cornstarch and water together to make a slurry. Slowly pour it into the pan while stirring gently.
  12. Simmer on low until the sauce thickens and turns glossy.
  13. Top with the green parts of the scallions. Serve hot with a warm bowl of rice.
